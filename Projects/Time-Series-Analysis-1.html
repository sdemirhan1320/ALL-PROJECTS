netdatats_dec<-decompose(netdatats,type = "multiplicative")
plot(netdatats_dec)

desea_data<-netdatats/netdatats_dec$seasonal
plot(desea_data, main="Deseasonalized Data")

acf(desea_data,main="Autocorrolation Plot")

detrend_and_desea_data<-desea_data/netdatats_dec$trend
plot(detrend_and_desea_data,main="Deseasonalized & Detrend Data")

acf(detrend_and_desea_data,na.action=na.pass,main="Autocorrelation Plot")

When we extract seasonality from the data, the trend stays only and because of the reason that trend is highly correlated it is can be easily seen from the deseasonalized autocorrelation function.

When the trend is extracted from the data, the acf of deseasonalized and detrend data stays below the significance range. This is indicative of a stationary series.Therefore, the numbers are purely random and the correlation can be theoretically zero, or at least insignificant.
